Where I'm From:
I'm a product of migration. I'm a Mexican-American first generation. I grew up in Chicago in a predominatnly Latinx neighborhood in the west side of the city. La Villita is a vibrant community that translates to "small village". This is the place I call home! I'm a native Spanish speaker and grew up with a strong Mexican culture integrated in my life.

How it all started

Allow me to set the setting, it is 2012 and tumblr is what everyone my age is using. Tumblr became a hub on the internet where teenagers like me found comfort. I 'rebloged' posts that I could relate to and edited my page (blog) to appeal to the aesthic of my content. I would look up 'tumblr layouts' then copy and paste the random words onto my editor, or I used the tumblr templates provided. One day, out of curiosity, I began to mess with the random words that seemed like a foreign language to me. I was messing with the HTML and CSS for my blog. I was fascinated by the way the computer followed my input. I started to google what the syntax meant, and taught myself the basics of HTML and CSS. This was my first exposure to Computer Science!

Why Diversity & Inclusion Matters to Me

I remember the exact moment in high school when I became aware that girls like me, did not code. As I left my first programming class, with my tote and heeled boots, I observed the line of white teenage boys that I shadowed out the door. This visual made me realize that I might spend my life working in a field where I may be one of few women in the room, maybe even the only woman of color. My career objectives greatly reflect upon the values I hold high in my life. These values are equality, learning and innovation. Diversity and inclusion are now conversations that we are starting have in STEM disciplines.
